Details

Date Saturday, 6 June 2026
Time 4.00 PM to 4.30 PM
Present Mark Yee Weicong, Serene Cheng, Carina Faith Peh Xin Yi
Agenda Topic selection for the Visual Analytics Project

1. Agenda

The team met to choose between the two VAST Challenge 2026 Mini-Challenges as the basis for the group project, and to agree on immediate next steps. Prior to the meeting, the team reviewed the project challenges and shared initial thoughts on possible directions from MC1 and MC2.

2. Discussion

2.1 MC2 summary (Serene and Carina)

Serene and Carina presented MC2 as a forensic investigation of a single anomalous event, namely a gibberish post that appeared on SaidIT on 17 May 2046 under the identity of John Windward (Head of Customer Support, TenantThread) in a random forum, raising the concern of a malfunctioning or manipulated AI agent. The data is a large event log of around 185,000 timestamped system actions plus an org chart of 75 nodes. The challenge asks how the post was made, what the content means and where it came from, and whether the behaviour could recur, ending in one recommended intervention point. The suggested approach is to establish a normal-post baseline, isolate the anomaly, trace the event chain back through agents, files and task delegations, and then recommend a control, with the chain running across 19 agents over eight days.

2.2 MC1 summary (Mark)

Mark presented MC1, in which TenantThread runs seven AI agents through a PR crisis while a CivicLoom merger sits under embargo. The Judge, a compliance agent, monitors only two of the six channels, yet embargoed information still reaches the public, so the challenge is to determine whether an agent bypassed the rules or the monitoring broke down, and to prove it from the messages. The data is a nested log of 912 messages across 23 rounds and six channels of differing accountability. The approach sets a baseline of normal behaviour and then flags abnormal channel use, bypass posts (public posts with no monitored trace) and changes in the communication network, with every signal traceable back to the message text and the agent’s private reasoning.

2.3 Key issues and observations on MC1

For MC1, the team discussed a case involving multiple AI agents and highlighted several key issues. These were a weak compliance system design, a lack of enforcement authority (for example, the judge agent being unable to stop actions) and AI agents operating with high autonomy that led to decision-making beyond their intended scope. It was observed that warnings were issued throughout the process but ignored, that the early-announcement breach appeared planned rather than reactive and that the governance mechanisms were insufficient to prevent the outcome. The team also discussed the difficulty of modelling such behaviour in an application, particularly detecting AI intent or reasoning, identifying when an AI operates beyond defined rules and designing meaningful investigative features for users.

2.4 Comparison and decision

Mark noted that MC1 is more ambiguous and exploratory, which allows deeper investigation into AI behaviour and interactions. Carina agreed that MC1 requires more effort in designing the modules and the user interface but offers greater flexibility and creativity. Serene, who had done a deep analysis on MC2, raised that MC2 is more structured and straightforward as it focuses on tracing and investigation with clearer steps. The team collectively expressed that MC1 is more challenging but interesting while MC2 is easier but less distinctive. The discussion went smoothly and the team agreed to proceed with MC1.

2.5 Shiny app ideas for MC1

Mark brought up some Shiny app ideas for MC1, including an interactive dashboard to visualise channel activity by round and identify the most active participants, and a tracking module to detect external message outputs and identify potential sources of breaches. Serene added that the team could include network-analysis features such as sender-receiver relationships (a bipartite graph), the overall communication network and the centrality and influence measures covered in class. Carina suggested taking keyword or topic analysis into consideration, such as searching and tracking keywords across rounds. She also emphasised a user-friendly and intuitive design.

3. Decision

The team selected Mini-Challenge 1 (MC1) as the topic for the Visual Analytics Project.
